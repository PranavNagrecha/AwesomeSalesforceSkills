# Gotchas — Slack Connect Patterns

Non-obvious Slack platform behaviors that cause real production problems in this domain.

## Gotcha 1: Message Deletion Is Not Bilateral

**What happens:** When a member of Organization A deletes a message in a Slack Connect channel, the message disappears from Organization A's view. However, Organization B still retains its copy of the message under Organization B's own retention policy. There is no cross-org delete propagation.

**When it occurs:** Any time a user or an automated retention policy deletes a message in a Slack Connect channel. This includes manual user deletion, admin bulk-delete, and retention policy auto-deletion.

**How to avoid:** Do not design compliance processes that rely on delete-to-remove as a bilateral control in Slack Connect channels. Contractually require both organizations to apply matching retention policies (where legally permissible) and document the asymmetry in your data governance register. If complete bilateral removal is a hard requirement, reconsider whether Slack Connect is the right collaboration medium.

## Gotcha 2: Channel Conversion to Slack Connect Exposes Prior History

**What happens:** Converting an existing internal Slack channel to a Slack Connect (externally shared) channel makes the channel's full prior message history visible to external members as soon as they join. There is no mechanism to selectively hide pre-conversion history.

**When it occurs:** Any time a workspace admin uses "Share this channel" on an existing channel rather than creating a new dedicated channel.

**How to avoid:** Always create a new, empty channel for Slack Connect. Archive the original internal channel if needed. Brief all stakeholders that internal discussion should continue in a separate private channel, not in the externally shared channel.

## Gotcha 3: Plan Downgrade Silently Removes DLP Coverage

**What happens:** If a partner organization downgrades from Enterprise Grid to Business+ mid-connection, their native Slack DLP rules stop applying to their members' messages in the shared channel. The channel itself remains active. Neither organization receives a notification that DLP coverage has changed.

**When it occurs:** Any time a partner organization changes their Slack subscription tier during the life of the Slack Connect channel relationship.

**How to avoid:** In regulated use cases, include a contractual clause requiring the partner to notify you of any Slack plan tier change. Establish a quarterly review process that confirms both organizations' plan tiers. For the highest-risk channels, require both organizations to independently operate third-party DLP alongside any native controls — third-party DLP survives plan changes.

## Gotcha 4: Invite Link Expires After 14 Days

**What happens:** The Slack Connect invite link generated by the internal admin expires exactly 14 days after creation. If the receiving organization's admin does not accept within that window, the link becomes invalid and a new one must be generated.

**When it occurs:** Very commonly when the invite is sent to a non-admin user (e.g., a project manager) who then forwards it to the correct Slack admin after the deadline. Also occurs when the receiving organization's admin is on vacation or has a change approval process that takes longer than two weeks.

**How to avoid:** Send the invite link directly to the external Slack admin's verified email. Confirm receipt and acceptance within 5 business days. If acceptance has not been confirmed by day 10, follow up before the link expires. Document the 14-day window in the project kickoff checklist.

## Gotcha 5: eDiscovery Export Scope Is Per-Workspace, Not Per-Channel

**What happens:** When legal requests a Slack export for a Slack Connect channel, the export produced by a single organization covers only that organization's members' messages. The complete conversation in the channel requires parallel exports from both organizations. Many legal and IT teams assume that exporting the channel from one workspace produces the full record.

**When it occurs:** Any litigation hold, regulatory examination, or eDiscovery request involving a Slack Connect channel. Also occurs during internal investigations where only one organization cooperates.

**How to avoid:** Document this limitation explicitly in every legal hold notice that involves a Slack Connect channel. Coordinate with the partner organization to produce a parallel export and merge by timestamp. Inform outside counsel that the exported record is half the conversation. For Enterprise Grid organizations, Slack's native Compliance Export produces a machine-readable JSON that can be merged by `ts` (Unix timestamp) fields.

## Gotcha 6: 250 External Organization Hard Limit

**What happens:** A single Slack Connect channel can include members from at most 250 external organizations. Attempts to add a 251st external organization will fail silently (the invite will appear to be sent but the organization will not be able to accept).

**When it occurs:** Large consortiums, industry associations, or multi-party contracting situations where many independent organizations need to participate in a single channel.

**How to avoid:** If more than 250 external organizations are anticipated, split into topic-specific sub-channels or consider an alternative collaboration platform. Track the current external org count in the channel's admin metadata before sending new invites.
